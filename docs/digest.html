Near-miss for Mexico’s parametric catastrophe bond on Friday’s M7.3 earthquake

After a magnitude 7.3 earthquake struck just off the southern coast of Mexico on Friday, analysis suggests this event was a near-miss for attaching the parametric trigger of one tranche of notes from the country’s World Bank facilitated IBRD CAR Mexico 2024 catastrophe bond transaction. Mexico has a robust natural disaster risk transfer and insurance program in place, at the heart of which sits its World Bank supported catastrophe bond issuances, as well as its traditional parametric insurance program. The Government of Mexico had secured $420 million of parametric disaster insurance protection against earthquakes and Atlantic hurricanes through the World Bank facilitated IBRD CAR Mexico 2024 catastrophe bond transaction, issued in early April 2024.

Cover Genius Raises $100M in Capital From Vista Credit Partners

Cover Genius, the insurtech for embedded insurance, said on July 15 it raised $100 million in capital, backed by Vista Credit Partners, a subsidiary of Vista Equity Partners. The raise ups the business to a $1.9 billion valuation, said Cover Genius, adding that it will use the new capital to deepen enterprise partnerships with enhanced integration, scale AI capabilities, and invest in platform scalability and strategic acquisitions. Cover Genius operates a B2B2C embedded protection platform that connects 200+ partners with 50+ global insurance carriers to protect over 70 million end customers at the point of sale across travel, retail, ticketing and logistics.

5. 🌪️ Clima & Catástrofes

World Bank starts work on up to $190m Nepal parametric quake catastrophe bond project

The World Bank has begun work on a proposed project to deliver a parametric earthquake catastrophe bond for Nepal, targeting US $80 million to US $190 million of fully-collateralized disaster risk financing from the capital markets for the South Asian country. The project envisages a parametric catastrophe bond that could provide around $80 million of coverage for Nepal in the event of a 1-in-20 year quake, up to $190 million for a 1-in-100 year earthquake occurring. The Nepal Catastrophe Bond Project appears to be in its nascent stages, with preparatory analysis looking at the feasibility and assessing the potential impact of delivery of a parametric earthquake cat bond for the country.
