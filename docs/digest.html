2. 🌪️ Clima & Catástrofes

Adaptation projects essential for narrowing global nat cat protection gap: Swiss Re

A new report from Swiss Re has suggested that the rising value of natural catastrophe-exposed assets, and the resulting increase in the absolute protection gap, continues to pose resilience risks, even though insurance cover has broadly kept pace with rapidly growing exposures, highlighting the need for targeted adaptation projects that reduce expected losses, lower physical vulnerability, support insurability and improve the economics of risk transfer. According to the reinsurer’s new report, the global natural catastrophe protection gap reached $424 billion in 2025, up from $395 billion a year earlier. At the same time, Swiss Re observed that, if the long-term 5–7% annual real-term growth trend in insured losses persists, global insured losses could reach $186 billion by 2030.

4. 🌪️ Clima & Catástrofes

EHAB Launches Parametric Weather Platform for Brokers

EHAB (London), a climate-risk technology company specializing in weather intelligence, operational climate risk and parametric insurance infrastructure, has launched WeatherWise Studio, a parametric weather structuring and workflow platform for brokers, insurers and reinsurers. EHAB says the platform combines more than 45 years of global weather history at approximately 9 km resolution with a library of industry-specific parametric structures for sectors including renewable energy, construction, logistics, agriculture, hospitality, commercial real estate and infrastructure. The platform, which has been in beta with selected broker partners since January 2026, is designed to let brokers move from an initial client discussion to a structured parametric submission in under 30 minutes, according to the company.
