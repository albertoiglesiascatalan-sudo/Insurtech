Gallagher Re estimates global insured cat losses at $46bn for H1’26, 28% below 10-year average

Insured losses from natural catastrophe events across the globe in the first half of 2026 are estimated to have reached USD $46 billion, down on last year’s $84 billion and 28% below the 10-year average of $64 billion, according to reinsurance broker Gallagher Re’s H1 2026 Natural Catastrophe and Climate Report. In addition, Gallagher Re noted that economic losses also fell in the first half of 2026 to $142 billion, which is 10% below the 10-year average. However, while global catastrophe losses in H1’26 have remained below average, Gallagher Re highlights the continuing evolution of global risk, highlighted by unprecedented early summer heat in Europe, and the emergence of El Niño conditions during June.

UK financial regulators to begin overseeing Critical Third Parties announced by Treasury

The Bank of England (the Bank), the Prudential Regulation Authority (PRA) and the FCA will start overseeing the first critical third parties (CTPs) on Monday 13 July 2026, following designation by the Treasury. Sarah Breeden, deputy governor for financial stability at the Bank, said: 'As critical third parties become increasingly embedded in the operations of financial institutions, they can introduce new forms of systemic risk. For the first time, the three regulators will jointly oversee these CTPs under a new, proportionate regime, focused on the resilience of the critical services they provide to the UK financial sector.
