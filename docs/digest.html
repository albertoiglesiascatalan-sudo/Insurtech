Cover Genius Raises $100M in Capital From Vista Credit Partners

Cover Genius, the insurtech for embedded insurance, said on July 15 it raised $100 million in capital, backed by Vista Credit Partners, a subsidiary of Vista Equity Partners. The raise ups the business to a $1.9 billion valuation, said Cover Genius, adding that it will use the new capital to deepen enterprise partnerships with enhanced integration, scale AI capabilities, and invest in platform scalability and strategic acquisitions. Cover Genius operates a B2B2C embedded protection platform that connects 200+ partners with 50+ global insurance carriers to protect over 70 million end customers at the point of sale across travel, retail, ticketing and logistics.

White House Unveils AI Clearinghouse for Cybersecurity Risks

The Trump administration is moving to strengthen coordination on artificial intelligence cybersecurity through a new industry clearinghouse to improve the detection and patching of network vulnerabilities, especially in open-source software. Still, it remains unclear if the White House’s more recent heavy-handed approach is a permanent feature of its AI policy and what that means for any new releases on the horizon. The clearinghouse, named Gold Eagle, was rolled out earlier this month by the Treasury Department, Department of Homeland Security and the Pentagon, in consultation with AI companies, according to National Cyber Director Sean Cairncross, who briefed reporters on the initiative July 14.
