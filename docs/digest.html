5. 💰 Inversión

July European SCS outbreak to potentially cause losses over a billion euros: Aon

Widespread severe convective storms (SCS) across Europe between July 10–17, 2026, will likely result in substantial economic and insured losses, with aggregate financial losses expected to reach at least hundreds of millions of euros and the potential for the sequence to become a billion-euro industry event as damage assessments continue, according to Aon’s recent Weekly Cat Report. According to the event details, between July 10-17, a strong ridge of high pressure dominated northwestern Europe between the British Isles and Scandinavia, trapping a moist, hot air mass over Western and Central Europe. In the report, Aon explained that the damage was primarily driven by hail, with additional impacts from strong winds and localised flash flooding, with most losses concentrated in France and Germany.
