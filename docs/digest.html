1. 🌪️ Clima & Catástrofes

KatRisk expands global catastrophe modelling capabilities with RED acquisition

KatRisk, a provider of catastrophic risk modelling software to the insurance and financial services industries, has acquired RED, a specialist provider of catastrophe models, including earthquake, flood, landslide, and wind risk models. “By bringing RED into the KatRisk family, we can extend our footprint into earthquakes and continue to raise the bar on the quality and speed of catastrophe modelling available to the market.” KatRisk’s existing strengths in flood, tropical cyclone wind, severe convective storm (SCS), and in the near future wildfire, are further enhanced with the addition of RED’s proven European earthquake platform and complementary climate peril models, the firm stated. The acquisition of RED reinforces KatRisk’s commitment to advancing innovation, deepening technical expertise, and expanding global reach.

Pioneer CAT Bond Fund nears $2bn AUM milestone, dedicated ILS fund assets over $5.2bn

The Victory Pioneer CAT Bond Fund is approaching a new milestone of $2 billion in assets under management, while the combined AUM of the dedicated mutual and institutional insurance-linked securities (ILS) funds managed by the Pioneer Investments team is now over $5.2 billion. Now, the latest reported data from the two dedicated mutual ILS funds portfolio managed by the Pioneer Investments team shows combined assets of just over $2.6 billion as of January 31st 2026.

5. ⚖️ Regulación

Eiopa plantea la posibilidad de un pool europeo para gestionar grandes catástrofes naturales

El primero sería un pool europeo de seguros frente a catástrofes naturales, financiado mediante primas basadas en el riesgo, que permitiría diversificar exposiciones entre países y tipos de peril. En este contexto, Eiopa y el Mecanismo Europeo de Estabilidad (ESM) han publicado un documento de discusión en el que analizan un posible enfoque europeo de reparto del riesgo para mejorar la gestión de los eventos naturales extremos. El documento no plantea un diseño cerrado, sino que busca alimentar el debate regulatorio y político sobre cómo afrontar de forma más coordinada el impacto financiero de las catástrofes naturales en Europa.
