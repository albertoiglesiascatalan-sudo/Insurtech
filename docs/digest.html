1. ⚖️ Regulación

Sompo launches new environmental strategy

Sompo Holdings has launched a new sustainability strategy called “SOMPO Earth Positive Actions,” expanding its previous climate-focused framework to address climate change, biodiversity, and the circular economy through what it describes as a “synergy approach.” The Japanese insurer said the strategy aims to support both environmental progress and the group’s long-term growth, while maintaining its target of achieving net-zero emissions by 2050. Sompo said approximately 70,000 employees across the group will work with customers, business partners, and local communities to implement the strategy. The initiative is built around three focus areas: regional action, global engagement, and environmental talent development.

2. 🌪️ Clima & Catástrofes

Mercury raises Cal fire reinsurance target to $125m-$175m with second Luca Re cat bond

Mercury General Corporation (Mercury Insurance), the California headquartered insurer, is now aiming to upsize its second 144A catastrophe bond sponsorship, with the target for fire reinsurance limit from the Luca Re Ltd. Mercury Insurance sponsored its debut 144A catastrophe bond in 2025, when it secured $150 million of California fire reinsurance protection from the capital markets from the first Luca Re 2025-1 deal. The Series 2026-1 cat bond notes that Luca Re is offering will provide Mercury with a roughly three-year source of collateralized reinsurance protection against wildfire and fire-following earthquake losses in the state of California, on an indemnity trigger and per-occurrence basis.

3. 🌪️ Clima & Catástrofes

Mexico doubles parametric catastrophe insurance to ~$575m at 2026 renewal

The government of Mexico has now finalised its main insurance protection for natural disasters and severe weather events for the coming year, doubling the size of its parametric catastrophe insurance arrangement to around US $575 million for 2026 into 2027. The government said this “reinforces its commitment to protecting the population from potential natural disasters,” while the doubling of the coverage expands its “capacity to protect public assets and provide timely assistance to the population affected by natural phenomena.” The largely parametric catastrophe insurance renewal runs from June 5th 2026 to May 5th 2027 and provides risk transfer protection against earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, hurricanes, and floods of medium to high severity. Mexico has been utilising parametric risk transfer in both insurance and catastrophe bond formats for many years, as the country has learned to appreciate the benefit of predictable and responsive, fast paying protection against natural disaster events.

Everest targets $530m of retro with two Kilimanjaro III Re catastrophe bond series

Everest Re, the global reinsurance unit of Everest Group, has returned to the catastrophe bond market in search of at least $530 million of multi-peril collateralized North America focused retrocession with three and four year tenure Kilimanjaro III Re Ltd.
